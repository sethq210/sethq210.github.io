The Story of the Boston Celtics

A data analysis of the 2022-2023 season

The Boston Celtics finished last season with 57 wins and 25 losses, the second best record in the NBA and the eastern conference. Here are charts that give a good summary of how well the team played throughout the season.

The Celtics were consistently good throughout the year. Their points per game remained relatively the same along with assists per game, field goal percentage, and three-point percentage. The biggest things that changed from 2021 to 2022 were rebounds per game and free throw percentage. The team's rebounds per game increased by nearly 3 rebounds a game, and the free throw percentage dropped by nearly 4%. The drop in free throw percentage in the second half of the season is something a team can easily counteract, so that is something the team needs to keep in mind heading into next season. The rebounding increase is a good sign, but is something that can be dictated by opponent rebounding prowess.

The Celtics were pretty dominant for most of the year based on their scoring margin, especially in late November and early December. Their rolling average scoring margin nearly reached +9 on December 7th, and then steadily went down and stuck to around 6, finishing at 6.5. Based on the chart, you can see the Celtics started strong, and while still were good, trailed off a bit as the season went on. That could be attributed to fatigue, so it will be interesting if the team follows the same pattern this coming season.

With a new look Celtics roster and Joe Mazzulla entering his second year as the team's head coach, it will be interesting to see if the Celtics continue to show signs of dominance and consistency throughout next season. If the Celtics can keep their rebounding up and maintain their high free throw percentage from early in the season, they should be even better next season. Combine that with the acquisition of former all-star Kristaps Porzingis, and the team can potentially have an even more dominant season and finish with the best record in the NBA and looking like the championship favorite heading into the playoffs.
